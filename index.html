Ch 1–4
Ch 1 — Nature & Significance of Management
Definition: Getting things done effectively & efficiently using human, physical & financial resources.
Koontz & O'Donnell "Art of getting things done through & with people in formally organised groups."
Effective = Doing the right things (achieve goal)  |  Efficient = Doing things right (min cost/time)
Management: Art + Science + Profession?
ArtScienceProfession
Personal skill, creativity, judgement; perfected via experience Systematised body; cause-effect; inexact/social science (less perfect) Well-defined knowledge, restricted entry, professional assoc (FICCI/CII), ethical code, service motive
⚡ Management is BOTH Art AND Science

Ch 2 — Principles of Management
Principles = guidelines to action → cause & effect → derive significance from UTILITY
Less than perfect — apply to human behaviour | Contingent — vary from situation to situation
F.W. Taylor — Scientific Management
American Mechanical Engineer ASME President Bethlehem Steel Co. (for Piece Rate Wage System)
Published: "Principles of Scientific Management" — 1911
Definition: "Knowing exactly what you want men to do and seeing that they do it in the best and cheapest way"

4 Principles:
  • Science, not Rule of Thumb — replace trial & error with scientific study
  • Harmony, not Discord — Mental Revolution; both mgmt & worker; 2 interests = 1
  • Cooperation, not Individualism — workers in mgmt decisions; reward suggestions
  • Development of each worker — worker well-being = business prosperity
Techniques:

Time StudyStandard time per task → determines no. of workers
Fatigue StudyFrequency of rest intervals
Differential Piece WageMore sophisticated = more savings; original purpose at Bethlehem
StandardisationPredetermined standards & norms for efficient resource use
Mental RevolutionComplete Δ in attitude of both workers & management
✅ Assembly line = discovered by Taylor → used by FORD for Model-T | Latest development in Sc. Mgmt = LEAN Manufacturing | Operations Research = developed in WW II
Taylor → Workers & Production efficiency | Focus on SHOP FLOOR

  4. Analysing deviations
  5. Taking corrective action
Measurement techniques: Performance observation | Sample checking | Performance reports

Ch 9 — Financial Management
Objective: Maximise Shareholders' Wealth
3 Financial Decisions: Investment Financing Dividend
A) Investment Decision (Capital Budgeting)
Capital Budgeting = Long-Term Investment Decision
Short-term Investment = Working Capital Decision
✅ R&D Programme & Advertising Campaign = ALSO Capital Budgeting Decisions
Working Capital:
LevelImpact
High Working Capital↑ Liquidity
Lower Working Capital↑ Reduces Profitability
B) Financing Decision
Determines overall cost of capital & financial risk.
Financial Leverage = Proportion of debt in overall capital
Operating Risk = Business Risk → depends on fixed operating costs
⚡ Cost of Debt is LOWER than cost of equity | Increase use of debt → likely to lower overall cost of capital (if cost of equity unaffected)
Trading on Equity: Company employs more cheaper debt to enhance EPS
Capital Structure (Cap Mix): D/E or D/(E+D)
Cap Employed = D + E | EBIT = Earnings Before Interest & Tax
⚡ ROI > Rate of Interest → Debt ↓ → EPS ↑ (Trading on Equity)
Cheapest source of finance = Retained Earnings | Equity → Riskless (in terms of repayment)
C) Dividend Decision
Stability of Dividend
Increase dividend only when CONFIDENCE that earning potential has gone UP (not just current year)
Stability of Earning
Company with stable earnings = better positioned to declare increased dividend
⚠ Stability of Dividend ≠ Stability of Earning
Financial Planning & Formulae
Financial Planning = typically 3–5 years | Starts with Sales Forecast

ROI = (EBIT / Capital Employed) × 100  |  Cap Emp = D + E
EPS = EAE / Number of Shares
ICR = EBIT / Interest
BEP = FC / (SP - VC per unit)
Cap Mix = D/E  OR  D/(E+D)
⚠ WRONG: ROI = Net Profit/Sales | CORRECT: ROI = EBIT/Capital Employed × 100
WRONG: ICR = Net Profit/Interest | CORRECT: ICR = EBIT/Interest
